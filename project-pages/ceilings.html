INVERSE REALITIES

SARAH LAWRENCE COLLEGE | 3D MODELING

Fall 2016

As I leaned back to allow the hairdresser to wash my hair, I stared at the ceiling. I was at a loss as to what to focus on for my 3D Modeling conference. I remembered being a kid and sitting upside down on the couch and imagining what it would be like to walk on the ceiling. Everything would hang straight up, lighting would be directed away from you, and there would be lots of strange obstacles.

There are also so many interesting ceilings that we don't pay much attention to because they're just above our normal range of sight. I decided I would try to model some ceilings that I came upon, and also took submissions. A cool circle of stucco in the middle of a ceiling and an interesting chandelier were sent to me by my dad, and I ended up modeling that and my own bedroom ceiling.

I was thinking about art that would be interesting upside down as well, and considered using one of those optical illusions that are an old lady one way and a young girl the other way or something. Then I remembered the Thatcher effect, "a phenomenon where it becomes more difficult to detect local feature changes in an upside-down face, despite identical changes being obvious in an upright face." So I used a Thatcher effect-ed picture of Margaret Thatcher.

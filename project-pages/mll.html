PHYSICAL COMPUTING MISCELLANEOUS PROJECTS

SARAH LAWRENCE COLLEGE | MOTORS, LIGHTS, & LOGIC

Spring 2016

I took a class called "Motors, Lights, & Logic" which was a physics class taught through physical computing with Arduino. This was my first experience applying my computer science interests and skills to something physical. It was immensely fun to create "real world" computer programs with sensor input rather than key presses, and physical outcomes to these. It allowed me to be creative with what I built, needing to make a physical structure to hold the Arduino and act out what I intended.

I made a game that was essentially "keep away" from the lumberjacks where the player controls a group of trees. I used vibrating motors to function as bees rustling in a flower. I collaborated with a classmate to use a projector's speaker to be the output for a piano program we wrote, which could also record and play back songs. For more information on all of these, and a few other projects and images, check out my blog.

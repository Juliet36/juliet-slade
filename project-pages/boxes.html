JULIET'S BOXES

SARAH LAWRENCE COLLEGE | MOTORS, LIGHTS, & LOGIC

Spring 2016

For my physical computing class, I made a series of modular cardboard games. They're a set of interactive boxes that can be controlled by the same "control box". The idea was to advance through the games, moving on only when you win one and get the password that unlocks the next box's game. They are designed so that, as long as it uses the same 10 pins on the Arduino (a mix of analog and digital pins), one could keep adding boxes to the series.

The three I ended up making were:

  • Touch Box: a pattern of lights flashes and the user must press the copper pads in the same order.
  • Box-It: like Bop it but a cardboard box. You can twist, push, and tilt it.
  • Dice Box: a game that recognizes which cell a dice lands in.
